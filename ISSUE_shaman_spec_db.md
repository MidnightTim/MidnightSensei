# [Bug] Shaman specs producing inflated and unreliable scores — Elemental and Restoration spec DB out of sync with Midnight 12.0

**Labels:** `bug` `spec-db` `shaman` `midnight-12.0` `fixed-in-1.3.8`
**Reported:** April 2026
**Fixed in:** v1.3.8

---

## Observed Behaviour

A Restoration Shaman was consistently producing scores at or near 100 (A+) on boss fights in a repeatable and easily reproducible fashion, without any apparent exceptional performance on the player's part. The scores were not isolated to a single fight or circumstance — they reproduced reliably across multiple encounters of varying content types and durations.

For reference, the scoring system is designed so that near-perfect scores require consistent cooldown usage, rotational spell coverage, and resource management aligned with the spec's expected playstyle. A 100 A+ on a healer should reflect clean cooldown timing, appropriate overheal management, and active maintenance of core healing spells. The scores observed did not correspond to that standard on inspection.

This prompted a full review of the Shaman spec database entries against the Midnight 12.0 spell list and talent tree.

---

## What Was Identified

### Restoration Shaman — Three Scored Cooldowns Did Not Exist

The Restoration entry in the spec database contained six `majorCooldowns` entries. Three of them were abilities that Blizzard removed from the Restoration Shaman kit in Midnight 12.0 as part of a broader spec simplification pass:

| Ability | ID | Status in Midnight 12.0 |
|---|---|---|
| Cloudburst Totem | 157153 | **Removed** — pruned from Restoration as part of simplification removing high-timing-skill buttons |
| Ancestral Guidance | 108281 | **Removed** — stripped from the game in patch 11.1.0 (February 25 2025) |
| Healing Tide Totem | 108280 | **Removed** — confirmed not present in Midnight 12.0 Restoration |

Additionally, Blizzard removed the following abilities from Restoration in the same pass (not previously tracked, confirmed not present): Earthen Wall Totem, Ancestral Protection Totem, Wellspring, High Tide, Tidebringer, Undulation, Master of the Elements, Mana Tide, Tide Turner.

**Impact on scoring:** The cooldown usage score is calculated across all tracked cooldowns. When three of six tracked cooldowns map to spells that do not exist in the player's spellbook, `IsPlayerSpell` returns false for all three at fight start and they are excluded from scoring silently. This reduced the effective cooldown pool to three abilities (Spirit Link Totem, Healing Rain, Ascendance), all of which a competent Resto Shaman uses correctly by default. With the denominator shrunk to only spells the player genuinely has, cooldown scores were routinely elevated to 100 even on unremarkable performance.

### Restoration Shaman — No Rotational Spell Tracking

The Restoration entry had no `rotationalSpells` defined at all. This meant the addon had no opinion whatsoever on whether core healing spells — Riptide, Chain Heal, Healing Rain — were being used. A Restoration Shaman who never cast Riptide once during a fight, or who filled GCDs entirely with Healing Surge rather than efficient AoE heals, would receive no corrective feedback. Activity score (GCD uptime) would still be high regardless of which spells filled those GCDs.

Combined with the inflated cooldown score above, this created a path to near-perfect scores with essentially no spec-specific requirements.

### Elemental Shaman — Multiple Incorrect Spell IDs

The Elemental entry contained several errors introduced by Midnight 12.0 ability changes and a data entry mistake:

| Entry | ID | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Flame Shock (rotational) | 188196 | **Wrong ID** — 188196 is Lightning Bolt. Flame Shock is 470411 in Midnight 12.0 |
| "Lava Burst variant" (rotational) | 60103 | **Wrong ability** — 60103 is Lava Lash (Enhancement Shaman ability) |
| Fire Elemental (cooldown) | 198067 | **Ability changed** — no longer a manual summon in Midnight 12.0; auto-generated by Ascendance |
| Primordial Wave (cooldown) | 375982 | **Ability removed** — pruned from Elemental in Midnight 12.0; role merged into Voltaic Blaze |

**Impact on scoring:** The Flame Shock tracking entry (ID 188196 / Lightning Bolt) would never match a Flame Shock cast — the player could maintain perfect Flame Shock uptime and receive zero credit. Conversely, Lightning Bolt is cast constantly, so the rotational "never used" check would never fire either. The net effect was that Flame Shock tracking was silently dead. The Lava Lash entry (60103) would never match any Elemental cast for similar reasons. Fire Elemental and Primordial Wave as removed abilities behaved the same as the Restoration case — excluded from scoring silently, reducing the cooldown denominator.

## What Was Fixed (v1.3.8)

**Restoration Shaman:**
- Cloudburst Totem, Ancestral Guidance, and Healing Tide Totem removed from `majorCooldowns`
- Surging Totem (444995) added as the new primary throughput cooldown — confirmed present in Midnight 12.0 Restoration talent tree (nodeID 94877)
- Unleash Life (73685) added to `majorCooldowns` as a pre-heal amplifier — confirmed nodeID 92677
- Call of the Ancestors (443450) added as `talentGated` cooldown — confirmed nodeID 94888 (VERIFY in game)
- `rotationalSpells` added for the first time: Riptide (61295), Chain Heal (1064), Healing Rain (73920) — all confirmed in Midnight 12.0 Restoration files. Healing Rain moved from `majorCooldowns` to `rotationalSpells` as it is a rotational maintenance spell, not a burst cooldown

**Elemental Shaman:**
- Flame Shock ID corrected to 470411 — confirmed in Midnight 12.0 Elemental Spell List
- Lava Lash (60103) removed from rotationalSpells
- Fire Elemental (198067) removed from majorCooldowns
- Primordial Wave (375982) removed from majorCooldowns
- Earthquake (462620), Elemental Blast (117014), Voltaic Blaze (470057), and Tempest (454009) added as `talentGated` rotational spells — all confirmed in Midnight 12.0 Elemental talent tree

## Prevention

The new **Talent Export** and **Spells Export** tools added in 1.3.8 (accessible via the Debug Tools window) snapshot the player's live spellbook and active talent tree to `CharDB` on every login and spec change. These can be exported and diffed directly against the uploaded Midnight 12.0 spell and talent list files to catch ID mismatches and removed abilities before they affect scoring.
